# Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

Welcome to this comprehensive course on **Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics**. This course bridges the gap between artificial intelligence theory and real-world robotic applications, preparing you to understand and work with the next generation of intelligent machines.

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI represents a fundamental shift in how we think about artificial intelligence. While traditional AI operates in the digital realm—processing text, images, and data—Physical AI extends into the real world, enabling machines to perceive, reason about, and interact with their physical environment.

Consider the difference between an AI that can identify a coffee cup in a photograph versus one that can actually pick up that cup, carry it across a room, and place it gently on a table without spilling. The second task requires Physical AI—intelligence that understands not just what things are, but how they behave, how to manipulate them, and how to navigate the complex, unpredictable real world.

## Course Structure

This 12-week journey takes you from foundational concepts to cutting-edge applications:

### Part 1: Foundations (Weeks 1-2)

**Week 1** introduces the core philosophy of Physical AI. You'll learn why intelligence and embodiment are deeply connected, and why robots that interact with the physical world face fundamentally different challenges than software running on a server.

**Week 2** explores how robots perceive the world. Just as humans rely on eyes, ears, and touch, robots use cameras, LiDAR, IMUs, and other sensors. You'll understand not just how these sensors work, but how robots combine information from multiple sources to build a coherent understanding of their environment.

### Part 2: Movement & Control (Weeks 3-4)

**Week 3** covers how robots move and interact with objects. You'll learn about motors, control systems, and the mathematics that describes robot motion. This isn't just about equations—it's about understanding how robots can move smoothly, precisely, and safely.

**Week 4** introduces ROS2 (Robot Operating System), the standard framework that connects all the pieces of a robot together. Understanding ROS2 is essential for anyone working with modern robots.

### Part 3: Intelligence (Weeks 5-6)

**Week 5** shows how machine learning transforms robotics. Instead of programming every behavior by hand, robots can learn from experience. You'll understand reinforcement learning, where robots learn by trying things and receiving feedback—much like how humans learn.

**Week 6** dives deeper into neural networks for robotics—how these systems learn to see, understand, and make decisions. You'll also learn about transfer learning, which allows robots to apply knowledge from one task to another.

### Part 4: Simulation (Weeks 7-8)

**Week 7** explores robot simulation—virtual worlds where we can test robot behaviors safely and quickly. Modern simulators are so realistic that robots can learn almost entirely in simulation.

**Week 8** tackles the "sim-to-real" challenge: how do we ensure that behaviors learned in simulation work in the real world? This is one of the most active research areas in robotics today.

### Part 5: Humanoid Robotics (Weeks 9-10)

**Week 9** focuses on humanoid robots—machines built in our own image. You'll understand the unique challenges of bipedal locomotion (walking on two legs) and why humanoid robots are so difficult to build.

**Week 10** covers how humanoids interact with the world and with us. Manipulation (using hands to interact with objects) and human-robot interaction are crucial for robots that work alongside people.

### Part 6: Advanced Topics (Weeks 11-12)

**Week 11** explores multi-robot systems and the critical questions of ethics and safety in robotics. As robots become more capable, these issues become increasingly important.

**Week 12** looks toward the future—emerging technologies, research frontiers, and how Physical AI will transform our world in the coming decades.

This course is designed for:

- **Students** curious about robotics and AI
- **Engineers** transitioning into robotics
- **Researchers** wanting a broad foundation
- **Anyone** fascinated by intelligent machines

No prior robotics experience is required. We assume basic familiarity with mathematics and physics at a high school level.

## Course Philosophy

We believe that true understanding comes from grasping underlying principles, not memorizing facts. Throughout this course, we emphasize:

- **Why** things work, not just how
- **Intuition** and physical reasoning
- **Connections** between different topics
- **Real-world applications** and examples
